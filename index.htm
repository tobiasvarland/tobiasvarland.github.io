Important Information

The Love Has No Labels movement says the following on its website:

Love Has No Labels is a movement to promote acceptance and inclusion of all people across race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, age and ability. We believe love is the most powerful force to overcome bias. We celebrate diversity and encourage people to come together because we know, together, we can create a more inclusive world.

If your values don't align pretty closely with that statement, please don't contact me about working together. I respect that everybody is different and you can choose how to operate your business, but I have no interest in working for a photographer who would discriminate based on anything mentioned in that statement.

Why Hire Me?

Finding the right second shooter can be difficult. Here are some reasons why I believe you may want to hire me:

  1. I'm experienced.
    I previously ran my own business and I've done wedding photography for well over 100 weddings and wedding videography for well over 50 weddings. I have experience shooting solo, shooting as part of a husband and wife team, and second shooting for a variety of other photographers and videographers.
  2. I'm professional.
    Having run my own business in the past, I understand what your wedding photography business means to you and I know my role as a second shooter. I dress in an understated but professional manner. I interact with guests and other vendors in a polite and kind manner. I take my role as a representative for your brand seriously. I don't smoke, vape, or consume alcohol during weddings. I don't flirt with or hit on guests or members of the wedding party. Most of these statements should be unnecessary, but I've written them because these are all things I've seen at weddings.
  3. I'm competent.
    Not only do I own my professional gear, I know how to use it. I'm a nerd at heart and I'm the guy who reads the manual that comes with my camera. I know about the different focus modes and different metering algorithms, how to shoot manual, how to use a flash (on or off camera), etc. In short, while there are always new things to learn, I'm not a newbie cutting my teeth on your brand's dime.
  4. I'm reliable & available.
    Because I'm no longer a business owner, I'm able to commit to wedding dates well in advance. I'm not going to cancel because an opportunity to book my own event comes along. Also, while it should go without saying, I have reliable transportation.
  5. I'm not your competition.
    I love that there is a spirit of community and mentorship in the wedding photography community, but I also know sometimes it can feel like you're training your competition when using a second shooter. As I've stated, my days as a business owner actively pursuing my own wedding commissions are over and I don't need photos from your weddings to build my portfolio.

My Gear

When I was a lead photographer, I would tell my clients that I had enough gear and backup gear that if something broke, they wouldn't know it. I see that as my responsibility as a wedding photographer.

I shoot on Nikon D800 cameras. I always bring at least two with me to weddings. When shooting photos, I always shoot on two memory cards at a time in order to create a realtime backup. All of my lenses are genuine Nikon lenses. I have primes (35 f/1.4, 50 & 85 f/1.8), zooms (24-70 & 70-200 f/2.8 (two of each)), and a macro lens (105 f/2.8).

In addition to cameras and lenses, I have pretty much any accessory I can imagine needing at a wedding: flashes, remote triggers, tripods, monopods, spare batteries, video light, etc. Like a boy scout, I believe in always being prepared.
